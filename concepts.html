Fundamentals

Web3 concepts made simple with comparison cards.

This page shows the key differences between legacy Web2 ideas and the new Web3 world in a visual, easy-to-read layout.

Web2 vs Web3

Web2

Centralized platforms control data and user accounts. Companies own the servers, and users rely on trust in the service provider.

Web3

Decentralized networks let users own data, identities, and value. Control shifts from a single company to shared blockchain protocols.

Ethereum vs Bitcoin

Bitcoin

A digital money network built for secure peer-to-peer value transfer and store of value.

Ethereum

A programmable blockchain that supports smart contracts, decentralized apps, and token systems.

Public Key vs Private Key

Public Key

A shared address used to receive funds and verify digital signatures. It is safe to publish.

Private Key

A secret credential that proves ownership. Losing it means losing access, and sharing it exposes funds to theft.

Blockchain vs Traditional Databases

Traditional Database

Controlled by one organization with fast updates and flexible permissions, but data can be changed or deleted.

Blockchain

A distributed ledger where participants agree on a permanent history. It is slower, but tampering is difficult and records are verifiable.
